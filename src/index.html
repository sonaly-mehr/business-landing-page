Drive a Culture of Workplace Recognition

Why Workplace Recognition Matters

Per a report from Apollo technical, employee recognition leads to a 31% boost in Business productivity.

More than 40% of Employed Americans

Feel that if they were recognized more often, they would put more energy into their work.

Better Retention and Reduced Hiring Costs

Lack of recognition and engagement contributed to 44% of employees changing jobs and quiet quitting.

Boosted Company Culture

Companies that prioritize workplace recognition report a 32% increase in employee satisfaction and a more positive company culture (Gallup, 2017)

Increased Job Satisfaction

Recognized employees are 63% more likely to stay at their current job within the next three to six months.

Enhanced Teamwork

Employees who feel recognized are more likely to collaborate with their colleagues, leading to a 26% increase in teamwork and cooperation.

Enhanced Company Reputation

Companies with strong workplace recognition programs enjoy a better reputation in their industry, as recognized employees are more likely to share their positive experiences with others, resulting in a 28% increase in positive word-of-mouth (Corporate Responsibility Magazine, 2019)

Recognize Co-Workers for

Special Occasions

Make employees feel valued and appreciated on work anniversaries, birthdays, farewells and career milestones. Welcome new joins and make them feel part of the team from day 1.

Promotions

Recognizing their hard work, dedication, and commitment to professional growth.

Wins

Encourage team members to give shout-outs to their colleagues for small wins, or other accomplishments, fostering a positive and supportive work environment.

Help and Acts of Kindness

Recognize helpful employees for their efforts in making it a better place to work.

Celebrate Team Goals

Acknowledge and praise the collective efforts of a team when they achieve their goals or complete significant projects.

Learning and Mentorship

Celebrate employees who dedicate time and effort to mentor and support their colleagues, sharing their knowledge and expertise, fostering a culture of continuous learning, and contributing to the professional growth of others within the organization.
